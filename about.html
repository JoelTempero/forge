Our Story

The journey of Forge Aotearoa - how we came to be and where we're headed.

The Forge Aotearoa Story

Forge Aotearoa emerged from a recognised need within the Presbyterian Church of Aotearoa New Zealand to develop and support pioneer leaders - those called to create new expressions of church and participate in God's mission in fresh ways.

Our Journey

How We Got Here

May 2020

A Vision Recognised

The Presbyterian Church of Aotearoa New Zealand identified pioneer leader development as an emerging priority. There was a growing recognition that new approaches were needed to equip those called to missional ministry.

2021

The First Coracle Gathering

Three Presbyterian church groups - Alpine Presbytery, PressGo, and the Knox Centre for Ministry and Leadership - organised "Coracle," a gathering in Hanmer Springs where pioneers came together to share their experiences and stories.

2021

Connection with Forge Scotland

During the Coracle gathering, the team connected with Alan McWilliam of Forge Scotland. This international connection helped shape the vision for what would become Forge Aotearoa, drawing on the wider Forge International network.

2021

Alpine Presbytery as Caretaker

Alpine Presbytery committed to becoming the "caretaker" for Forge Aotearoa. This was a natural fit, as the presbytery had already developed several missional initiatives and had a focus on training lay leaders.

2022

Pioneer Course Launched

The pilot Pioneer Course was launched, offering spiritual formation and training for those sensing a call to create new expressions of church. This marked a significant milestone in the practical outworking of the Forge Aotearoa vision.

Today

Growing Network

Forge Aotearoa continues to grow, connecting pioneers across the country, hosting gatherings like Haerenga Tapu, and offering ongoing programs like Exploring Wayfinding and Wayfinding Spirituality.

Foundation

Building on Strong Foundations

Prior to Forge Aotearoa's establishment, Alpine Presbytery had already developed several missional initiatives that laid the groundwork for this new venture.

These included the Prestons New Mission Seedling, Hanmer Springs Wellbeing Centre, and the East Side Project. The presbytery also supported missional work within existing congregations and had a particular focus on training lay leaders.

This experience and commitment to grassroots missional work made Alpine Presbytery well-positioned to nurture Forge Aotearoa's development.
